01 · The Problem

The $5M Leak

Rover estimated more than 20% of potential marketplace revenue was being lost to off-platform bookings — relationships that started on Rover but completed elsewhere, outside Rover's fee structure.

I led design on two complementary initiatives: a cross-platform phone number blocking system and an in-product Community Guidelines modal — addressing both the means and the motivation to leave the platform.

Design bar we set
Not chasing a headline lift. The bar was "do not hurt booking rates." Keeping people in the product is only worth it if they can still successfully book.
Frame 01

Community Guidelines modal in three trigger states: existing users (next login), new owners (first request), new sitters (post-onboarding). Annotated to show the required "Got it" confirmation and re-appearance logic.

The modal had to educate without feeling like a blocker.

02 · Two Interventions

Phone Blocking + Community Guidelines

Phone blocking: any personal number shared before a booking was intercepted across all surfaces. On app/web, a modal explained why. On SMS, numbers were masked and both parties received an automated explanation. Once a booking was confirmed between that pair, the block lifted.

Community Guidelines modal: reached sitters and owners at critical moments. Required explicit "Got it" confirmation. Reappeared if the app was closed without confirming.

Special rules
Rover support numbers always allowed. Relay numbers blocked from re-sharing. Cross-surface consistency (iOS, web, SMS) required coordination across multiple engineering teams.
Frame 02

Phone blocking flow across all three surfaces — app interception modal with "Edit message" CTA, web inline warning, and SMS masking notification. Shows unified but surface-appropriate execution.

Designing the same intervention across three technically different surfaces was the central challenge.

03 · Impact

Neutral Broadly, Positive Where It Counts

Across the marketplace, booking rates stayed neutral — we didn't make it harder to successfully book. In the high-risk segments for diversion, results were clear.

When both sides had seen Community Guidelines, booking odds were 3.5% higher than control. Phone blocking contributed an estimated $3.6M annual upside. Community Guidelines contributed $2.2M annually in activated in-app bookings.
