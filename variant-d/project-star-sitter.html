01 · Overview

The Problem Space

Rover already had work in motion around successful sitters: onboarding best practices, booking walkthroughs, a Sitter Insights dashboard. Despite that, three gaps remained: owners couldn't identify the most reliable sitters in search; sitters saw metrics but lacked motivation to change behaviour; and off-platform diversion was a strategic revenue leak.

Star Sitter was built as a third lever — a provider-level status program that gamifies behaviours, directs demand to reliable supply, and ties status to on-platform repeat behaviour.

My role on this project
Lead product designer from first concept through global rollout — end-to-end status model, owner and sitter surfaces, and the explainability layer in Sitter Insights.

02 · Phases 1–2

Foundation & Beta

We launched Sitter Insights first — establishing the mental model before introducing status. Key metrics: response rate, booking rate, calendar updates, cancellations. A small positive improvement in booking rate (+0.6%) confirmed sitters responded to visibility.

The beta introduced provider-level, non-tiered status. Badge surfacing on search cards and profiles, plus an owner filter. The badge shifted owner behaviour meaningfully — but CX sentiment surfaced early cracks around edge cases.

Frame 01

3 annotated screens: Star Sitter badge on search result cards; the "Only show Star Sitters" owner filter; and the Sitter Insights qualification criteria panel.

The badge and filter worked in tandem to shift owner search behaviour.

03 · Phases 3–4

Understandable & Fair

Usability tests showed sitters skimmed past key rules. We tightened copy to use direct, instructive phrasing, pulled core rules into scannable bullets, and added a prominent "Check your progress" CTA.

Three major fairness changes: moved to recent activity windows; formalised a grace period for sitters who narrowly miss one metric; and fixed a Contact More Sitters bias that was unfairly penalising booking rates.

Frame 02

Affinity map from 6 moderated sessions — 3 clusters: qualification confusion (what counts toward the badge?), fairness concerns (low-activity vs. long-tenure), and competitive motivation.

Research synthesis that shaped the Phase 3–4 fairness interventions.

04 · Phase 5 · Impact

Rolling Status & Outcomes

Moving to a rolling model eliminated quarterly batch refreshes, predictable CX ticket spikes, and a fragile manual process. Sitters earn Star Sitter as soon as they meet criteria. A three-month grace period handles drops below criteria.

The Sitter Insights UX simplified dramatically: no more qualification period dropdowns, a single real-time status view. Better model, less complexity.
