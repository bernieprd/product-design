# Alt Monetization Email & Landing Page Content Study Report

**URL:** https://roverdotcom.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/DSN/pages/5658936684  
**Author:** David Quiroz | **Last modified:** Feb 02, 2026  
**Stakeholders:** Nil Bertran, Defne Kavas, Bernardo Prudêncio, Kathryn Mailler, Lizeth Herrera

---

## Summary

A moderated study with 12 Canadian Rover sitters evaluating the comprehension of content in the alt monetization email announcement and informational landing page. Goal: identify areas affecting understanding of the tiered system and the potential to earn more with repeated bookings with the same client.

**Participants:**
- 12 total, Canadian Rover sitters
- Mix of mostly Established sitters (~2+ years with repeat clients) and New sitters (~1 year or less)
- Booking volume ranged from 1 to 30 clients per month

---

## TL;DR Key Findings

- Overall, most content in the email and landing page was generally understood. The potential to earn more through a pilot limited to certain markets and a tiered system came across to all participants.
- The email mostly elicited curiosity, but had two areas causing confusion for some participants: the role of the client, and implied lower fees.
- Several participants fixated on the details of the tiered system interactive example and had difficulty interpreting it.
- There's a risk that users may skip the FAQs.

---

## Areas in Need of Improvement

### Finding 1 — Email: Role of the client (5 of 12 confused)

The sentence "Plus, see if your existing clients have already unlocked higher earnings!" in the "How it works" section implied to 5 of 12 participants that the client was directly involved and benefited from the tiered system as well. Frequent use of "client" combined with this sentence made it less clear who was financially benefiting.

**Consider:** Reinforcing that the sitter is the center of the tiered incentive and its main financial benefactor.

---

### Finding 2 — Email: Implied lower fees not explicit

Some participants wanted to know how the pilot would change their current situation. It was generally implied there's potential for higher earnings, but it wasn't necessarily clear if that meant lower fees and how their earning potential would be different compared to current earnings.

**Consider:** Mentioning explicitly in the email that advancing to higher tiers means lower Rover fees.

---

### Finding 3 — Landing page: Interactive tier example confusing

The "Make progress with every dollar" section had two parts: (1) a description of the tiers, and (2) an interactive example. Most participants found part 1 clear; many had difficulty with part 2.

Participants wanted to see threshold numbers ($350, $999, $1,000+) represented in the progress bar, and were observed scrolling up to refresh their understanding of those thresholds. They understood they were making forward progress through the tiers but were confused by how colors represented progress (past/present/future) and the presence of pending dollar amounts.

**Consider:** Displaying threshold numbers in the progress bar to reinforce what amounts are needed to advance. Simplifying the visuals for the interactive example.

---

### Finding 4 — Landing page: FAQs being skipped

During free exploration, several participants reached the FAQ section and abruptly stopped or scrolled back up to revisit other sections. There's a risk that users effectively skip FAQs.

Most relevant FAQs to sitters:
- "What if I provide multiple services or care for multiple pets for the same client?"
- "Do past bookings count toward my progress?"
- "Will clients know about or be affected by my relationship tier?"

**Consider:** Adding visual elements to make FAQs more appealing, or showing answers by default rather than requiring expansion.

---

## Other Findings

### Finding 5 — "See your clients" CTA placement

The "See your clients"/"Do you already have client relationships?" CTA appears before tier information, allowing users to skip important context. 

**Consider:** Relocating the CTA to below the tier information section, so sitters encounter the core explanation before jumping to their client list.

---

### Finding 6 — Tier 3 permanence unclear

A few participants questioned whether Tier 3 status was permanent or would go away if there was a significant gap between bookings — even after reading content intended to address this question.

**Consider:** Reinforcing the notion that Tier 3 status is not lost once reached. Add explicit language in the interactive example: *"From now on, it doesn't matter what service [client] books or how much time passes between bookings — every future booking is at [fee]%."*

---

## Concept Comprehension Scores

Participant self-ratings (1–5 scale, 4 or 5 = "understood"): No participants rated any concept with a strong lack of understanding (rating of "1"). Majority of participants rated concepts at 4 or 5 (green zone).
