## 1. Objective

Define and operationalize a UX measurement framework to monitor sitters' perceptions, comprehension, and experiences following the rollout of the Relationship-based Graduated Sitter Fee model in Canada.

This framework aims to:
- Detect early signals of confusion, friction, or dissatisfaction
- Track the evolution of sentiment and understanding over time
- Inform decisions across product, lifecycle communication, and monetization strategy
- Complement behavioral and business metrics with attitudinal and qualitative insight

## 3. Surveys Overview

### Reactive Feedback Survey (Landing Page + Email)

**Type:** Ongoing, self-selected feedback  
**When:** Immediately during rollout  
**Metrics:** CSAT (high-level sentiment), Open-ended qualitative feedback  

**What we gain:**
- Early detection of issues before they scale
- Identification of unexpected concerns or misinterpretations
- Language sitters use to describe the change (gold for comms)

**Limitations:** Not representative; biased toward extreme opinions  
**Best use:** Signal detection, not decision-making

Results available on SurveyMonkey (English and French dashboards).

---

### In-Product Survey (Payments Page)

**Type:** Continuous intercept (web + app)  
**When:** During real interaction with the payments page

**Metrics collected:**

*Clarity:* "How clear is it to you how your earnings on Rover are calculated?"
- Very unclear / Somewhat unclear / Neutral / Somewhat clear / Very clear

*CSAT:* "How satisfied are you with your experience as a sitter on Rover?"
- Very dissatisfied / Somewhat dissatisfied / Neutral / Somewhat satisfied / Very satisfied

**What we gain:**
- Contextual insight tied to real usage
- Ability to track trends over time
- Early detection of UX or comprehension breakdowns

**Best use:** Monitor comprehension + experience in real usage

---

### Post-Launch Email Survey (Q3 — WIP)

**Type:** One-off structured measurement (potential merge with Sitter Satisfaction Survey)

**Core constructs:** Comprehension, Business Volume Expectations, Informational Transparency, Perceived Organizational Support, Fees Fairness

**Draft questions:**
1. How satisfied are you with your experience as a sitter on Rover?
2. How clear is it to you how your earnings are calculated? (perceived clarity)
3. In your own words, how are your fees calculated? (real comprehension)
4. Have you noticed any changes in Rover fees as you continue working with the same clients?
5. Rover meets my expectations for the amount of business I receive
6. Rover provides me with sufficient communication about updates to the app, website, and policies
7. The fees charged by Rover are fair

**Best use:** Strategic evaluation, not monitoring

---

## 4. Sample Design

- **Reactive feedback survey:** Limited to Canadian sitters in the experiment group — capturing reactions from users directly exposed to the new fee model communications and landing page.
- **One-off email survey:** Includes Canadian sitters both in and outside the experiment, enabling comparison between exposed and non-exposed groups on key attitudinal outcomes.
- **In-product survey:** Available to all Canadian sitters interacting with the relevant product surfaces. Experimental group comparisons handled analytically (not at sampling stage) by segmenting respondents after collection.
