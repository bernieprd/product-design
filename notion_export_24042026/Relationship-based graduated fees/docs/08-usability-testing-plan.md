## Background

We are planning to launch a relationship based graduated sitter side fee model in Canada. We believe adapting our fee structure is a necessary (but not sufficient) step towards increasing take per relationship by incentivizing providers to grow their relationships on Rover and reward the ones who do so with more earnings.

## Goals

In this study we want to validate the UX that will support this new fee structure. Primarily we want to get a read on comprehension for both new and existing sitters. In addition, we hope to get a read on discoverability of communication touch points and key task completion.

## Learning Objectives

### Program level

**Awareness of Fee Changes:** Evaluate whether the communication touchpoints successfully create awareness of the fee changes among sitters.

* **Success:** Sitters recognize and recall the fee change after exposure to any provided touchpoints (TBC).
* **Failure:** Sitters remain unaware of the fee changes, either because they did not see or did not retain the information.  

**Visibility:** Assess whether sitters notice the fee-related messages across different communication channels (TBC).

* **Success:** Sitters easily find and acknowledge the notifications, callouts, and messages within the product.
* **Failure:** Sitters overlook or struggle to locate the fee-related touchpoints, reducing the effectiveness of the communication.  

**Comprehension:** Determine whether sitters understand the fee change details and their impact on earnings.

* **Success**: Sitters correctly explain the changes (the more they book a client the more they'll earn with that client) and their impact.
* **Failure**: Sitters misunderstand or misinterpret the fee structure, are uncertain about their earnings, or confuse it with other pricing models.  

**Perceived Relevance:** Measure how valuable and relevant sitters find the fee-related messaging.

* **Success:** Sitters feel the messages are clear, timely, and necessary, helping them make informed decisions about pricing and earnings.
* **Failure:** Sitters perceive the messages as unnecessary, redundant, or not applicable to their situation, leading to disengagement.  

**Retention & Recall:** Assess whether sitters retain and recall information about the fee changes after initial exposure.

* **Success:** Sitters remember the details of the fee reduction and can explain it correctly when asked at a later point.
* **Failure:** Sitters forget or misremember the information, requiring repeated exposure to understand it.

### Relationship level

**Discoverability:** Assess whether sitters are able to find the relevant information to understand what earnings level applies to a given client

* **Success:** Sitters easily navigate to the given client relationship page
* **Failure:** Sitters can't find the relationship page and have to be guided to it

**Comprehension:** Determine whether sitters understand earnings level that applies to a given client and why

* **Success**: Sitters correctly explain how much they expect to earn with a given client (in %)
* **Failure**: Sitters misunderstand or misinterpret the information provided or are uncertain about applicable earning level with a given client.

### Service and rates level

**Discoverability:** Assess whether sitters are able to find the relevant information to understand the new model when setting their rates

**Comprehension:** Determine whether sitters understand the model well enough so that it doesn't get in the way of setting their rates

### Booking level

**Discoverability:** Assess whether sitters are able to find the relevant information to understand how much they are earning from a given request and why

* **Success:** Sitters easily find the information to understand how much they'll earn from a given request and why
* **Failure:** Sitters don't find the information that explains how much they'll earn from a given request or why

**Comprehension:** Determine whether sitters understand earnings level that applies to a given client and why

* **Success:** Sitters easily understand how much they'll earn from a given request and why
* **Failure:** Sitters don't understand how much they'll earn from a given request or why
