01 / PROBLEM

Same fee.
Very different value.

A flat 20% on booking 1 vs. booking 20 treats every transaction as if Rover's contribution is identical. It isn't.

The fee is the same. Rover's actual contribution isn't.

03 / WHAT WE TRIED

The blunt tool
didn't work.

A 5% fee test in ES+GB — flat reduction for daytime services — was the first hypothesis: lower the fee, reduce diversion. Simple. But the results were clear.

WHAT WE LEARNED

No meaningful change in overall diversion. Material financial risk if scaled. Just lowering the fee is a blunt tool — it doesn't target the right relationship moments.

Model A New vs. Repeat

Lower fee on all repeat bookings, flat on all first bookings.

✓
Model B · Selected Relationship-Based GBV

Fee starts higher per client pair, drops as cumulative GBV hits milestones.

✓
Model C Account-Based GBV

Fee starts higher overall, drops as sitter's total Rover earnings hit annual milestones.

✓
COMPREHENSION

High mental model match. Sitters track clients, not annual totals. 'This client, this relationship.'

GAMING RISK

Moderate. GBV-based threshold mitigates booking-split gaming vs. count-based thresholds.

VERDICT

Best match to sitter mental models. Testing first in Canada.
