01 · overview

The Problem

Rover had work in motion around successful sitters — onboarding best practices, booking walkthroughs, a Sitter Insights dashboard. Despite that, three gaps remained: owners couldn't identify the most reliable sitters in search; sitters saw metrics but lacked motivation to change behaviour; and off-platform diversion was a large strategic revenue leak.

Star Sitter was built as a third lever: a provider-level status program that gamifies behaviours we care about, directs demand to our most reliable supply, and ties status to on-platform repeat behaviour.

Role & scope

As lead product designer, I co-led the design from first concept through global rollout and major iterations — including the end-to-end status model, owner and sitter surfaces, and the "explainability" layer in Sitter Insights.

02 · phase 1–2

Foundation & Beta

We launched Sitter Insights first — metrics like response rate, booking rate, calendar updates, and cancellations — establishing the mental model: "this is where I understand how my business is doing."

With the data foundation in place, we introduced Star Sitter as a beta in limited markets. Key design choices: provider-level, non-tiered status; qualification based on five metrics; badge surfacing on search cards and profiles; and an owner filter.

Early signals & friction

The owner-side split test showed the badge shifted behaviour meaningfully. But CX sentiment surfaced early cracks: confusion around booking rate, and pain around edge cases like long-tenured but low-activity sitters.

[ wireframe ]

Research synthesis: affinity map from moderated usability sessions with 6 sitters showing 3 clusters — qualification confusion, fairness concerns around low-activity sitters, and competitive motivation for the badge.

03 · phase 3–4

Understandable & Fair

Usability tests showed sitters skimmed past key rules on the landing page. We tightened copy to use direct, instructive phrasing, pulled core rules into scannable bullets, and added a prominent "Check your progress" CTA.

On tracking, we reshaped the Sitter Insights tab: clearer distinction between past snapshots and current-period metrics; a simple status message; and tiered "Learn more" patterns for the hardest concepts.

Three major fairness changes

Moved from lifetime to recent activity (5 recent clients, 2 repeat clients in 6 months). Formalised legacying — sitters who met criteria last period but miss one metric keep the badge for one grace period. Fixed a Contact More Sitters bias that was unfairly penalising booking rates.

04 · phase 5

Rolling Status & Impact

Quarterly refreshes gave stability at launch but created predictable CX ticket spikes around refresh days and a fragile manual batch process. We moved to a rolling model — sitters earn Star Sitter as soon as they meet criteria, with a three-month grace period on dropping below.

This let us dramatically simplify Sitter Insights UX: no more qualification period dropdowns, a single real-time picture of where sitters stand.
