01 · Origin

From Hackathon to Global Feature

Sitters set a simple circular radius — easy to build, but blind to real geography. Sitters told us: "I'm getting requests within my radius that are practically impossible." Owners saw sitters as available, only to be declined because the trip was too far.

This started as a Maker Days hackathon: could we replace the straight-line circle with a polygon that reflected how sitters actually move through cities? That prototype became the product.

"We started with the hypothesis that road-based polygons feel more intuitive than circles — and sitters confirmed it immediately."

Research finding, moderated prototype sessions

02 · Research

How Sitters Think About Distance

In moderated sessions, sitters reacted to their areas being redrawn, adjusted distance/time and travel mode, and talked through how far they'd go for different service types.

Three design anchors: sitters think in both miles and minutes; irregular road-based shapes feel more intuitive; and they want different areas per traveling service — wider for house sitting, more local for walks in dense cities.

The unified model
Distance (1–100 km/miles) or estimated travel time (1–60 min), with walking/cycling/driving support. House sitting, drop-ins, and walks each get their own service area. Existing radius backfilled automatically — no sitter starts from scratch.

03 · Results

Better Matching, Better Conversion

Two geographic A/B tests validated the approach. Both waves showed better matching and strong sitter adoption — so we committed to global rollout rather than extending a 50/50 split.

Search-to-request improved by +2–3% relative, search-to-book by +1.6–2.8% relative. Match quality improved by ~7%. New revenue per searcher was positive in both geo sets — around +5–6% in US markets.

The conversion trade-off
A small initial dip (–1–2%) in new-owner conversion: stricter "outside service area" behaviour. But the sitters who showed up were closer and more likely to accept. A fair trade for better long-run quality.
