01 · Overview

THE $5M LEAK

Rover estimated more than 20% of potential marketplace revenue was being lost to off-platform bookings — relationships that started on Rover but completed elsewhere.

Two complementary initiatives: a cross-platform phone number blocking system and an in-product Community Guidelines modal. Together: reduced the ability and incentive to move conversations off Rover.

Design bar
We set the bar as "do not hurt booking rates" — not chasing a headline lift. Keeping people in the product is only worth it if they can still successfully book.

COMMUNITY GUIDELINES MODAL

Three trigger states side by side: existing users (next login), new owners (first request), new sitters (post-onboarding). Annotated to show the required "Got it" confirmation and re-appearance logic.

02 · What We Shipped

TWO INTERVENTIONS

Phone blocking: any personal number shared before a booking was intercepted. On app/web, a modal explained why and returned users to edit their message. On SMS, numbers were masked with an automated explanation.

Community Guidelines modal: sitters and owners reached at their most relevant moment. Users had to explicitly confirm — "Got it" — and the modal reappeared if dismissed by closing the app.

Special rules & edge cases
Rover support numbers always allowed. Relay numbers blocked from re-sharing. The cross-surface consistency (iOS, web, SMS) required close coordination with multiple engineering teams.
[ NOT YET FILMED · WF-02 ]

CROSS-SURFACE BLOCKING FLOW

Phone blocking flow across all three surfaces — app interception modal with "Edit message" CTA, web inline number-sharing warning, and SMS masking notification. A unified but surface-appropriate experience.

03 · Impact

NEUTRAL BROADLY, POSITIVE WHERE IT COUNTS

Across the marketplace, booking rates stayed neutral. In the high-risk segments: when both sides had seen Community Guidelines, booking odds were 3.5% higher than control.

Phone blocking contributed an estimated $3.6M annual upside. Community Guidelines contributed $2.2M annually in activated in-app bookings.
