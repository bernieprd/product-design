01 · Overview

THE PROBLEM

Rover had work in motion around successful sitters but three core gaps remained: owners couldn't identify the most reliable sitters in search; sitters saw metrics but lacked motivation to change behaviour; and off-platform diversion was a strategic revenue leak.

Star Sitter was built as a third lever — a provider-level status program that gamifies behaviours we care about, directs demand to our most reliable supply, and ties status to on-platform repeat behaviour.

My role
Lead product designer from first concept through global rollout — end-to-end status model, owner and sitter surfaces, and the explainability layer in Sitter Insights.

02 · Phase 1–2

FOUNDATION & BETA

We launched Sitter Insights first, establishing the mental model before introducing status. The beta introduced provider-level, non-tiered status: badge surfacing on search cards and profiles, plus an owner filter.

The badge shifted owner behaviour meaningfully — but CX sentiment surfaced early cracks around qualifying edge cases.

Early signals
Confusion around booking rate calculation. Pain around long-tenured but low-activity sitters who felt unfairly excluded. These shaped Phases 3–4 entirely.
[ NOT YET FILMED · WF-02 ]

RESEARCH SYNTHESIS

Affinity map from 6 moderated usability sessions — 3 insight clusters: qualification confusion, fairness concerns around low-activity sitters, and competitive motivation for the badge.

03 · Phase 3–5

UNDERSTANDABLE, FAIR, ROLLING

We tightened copy, added a "Check your progress" CTA, and made three major fairness changes: recent activity windows, a grace period for sitters who narrowly miss, and fixing a booking-rate bias.

Phase 5 moved to a rolling model — sitters earn Star Sitter as soon as they meet criteria. No more quarterly batch refresh, no more CX ticket spikes. A dramatically simpler Sitter Insights UX.

Before/after UX detail
Quarterly model: qualification period dropdowns, manual batch refresh, fragile ops process. Rolling model: single real-time status message. Same information, radically less complexity.
